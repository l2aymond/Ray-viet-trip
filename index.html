Vietnam 9-Day Itinerary

Ho Chi Minh City & Da Nang (Dec 20 - Dec 28)

Critical Pre-Departure Briefing

1. Connectivity Strategy (eSIM)

The plan to buy a physical SIM at the airport carries a high risk of scams (overcharging, faulty SIMs). This could leave you without data when you need it most for booking a Grab.

Recommendation: Pre-purchase a Viettel network eSIM from a trusted platform like Klook before you travel. This is the most secure and efficient strategy, ensuring you have data the moment you land.

2. Climate Expectation (Da Nang)

December in Da Nang (20-25Â°C) is not a beach-swimming month. Your notes on "rougher waters and stronger winds" are correct. Swimming will likely not be feasible or safe.

Recommendation: Reframe your time at My Khe Beach as a scenic walk or photographic opportunity. Pack a light jacket or sweater, as it will be necessary for beach walks and evenings.

3. Budget Mismatch (Da Nang Seafood)

There is a critical mismatch between your restaurant budget (150k-400k VND) and your selected restaurants. My Hanh Seafood and Be Man Seafood are Michelin-listed, with actual costs closer to 500k-1,000k VND per person.

Recommendation: Treat these meals as "splurge" experiences and adjust your daily budget accordingly, or, seek out standard, non-Michelin-listed local restaurants which will fit your 150k-300k VND budget.

4. Health & Safety Protocols

  • Theft Protocol (HCMC D1 / Ben Thanh): Do not use back pockets. Wear a cross-body bag on your front. Do not walk and text with your phone in hand near the street; motorbike snatching is a real risk.
  • Insect Protocol (Cu Chi Tunnels): This is non-negotiable. Wear long pants and closed-toe shoes. Apply strong mosquito repellent *before* you get on the bus and bring it to re-apply.
  • Food Safety Protocol: Use the 3-step test for street food: Is it busy (especially with locals)? Is it hot (cooked-to-order)? Is it clean (observe the vendor)? Do not drink tap water.

Ho Chi Minh City (Days 1-4)

Day 1 (Dec 20): Arrival & D1 Orientation

Arrive at SGN, transfer to District 1 accommodation.

Recommendation: Using the Grab app for your airport transfer is the most reliable and transparently-priced option.

Day 2 (Dec 21): Historical Context

Morning tour to Cu Chi Tunnels, followed by an afternoon visit to the War Remnants Museum. Relax at Nguyen Hue Walking Street in the evening.

Watch Out For: This is an emotionally grueling day. Both sites present graphic and somber content. The schedule is tight; you must book the earliest possible Cu Chi tour (7:30/8:00 AM) to have time for the museum (last entry 5:00 PM).
Recommendation: The Insect Protocol (long pants, repellent) is mandatory for the Cu Chi Tunnels.

Day 3 (Dec 22): D1 Landmark Analysis

Walking tour of Reunification Palace, Notre Dame, Central Post Office, Jade Emperor Pagoda, Ben Thanh Market, and Bitexco Skydeck for sunset.

Watch Out For: Your original plan is flawed. The Reunification Palace closes for a 1.5-2 hour lunch break (approx. 11 AM - 1 PM). You will likely miss it if you don't go first.
Recommended Sequence:
  1. 9:00 AM: Reunification Palace (avoids closure)
  2. 11:00 AM: Walk to Notre Dame (exterior) & Central Post Office
  3. 12:00 PM: Grab to Jade Emperor Pagoda
  4. Afternoon: Grab to Ben Thanh Market (Apply Theft Protocol!)
  5. 4:30 PM: Arrive at Bitexco Tower for sunset view.

Day 4 (Dec 23): Transfer to Da Nang

Straightforward transfer day. Fly from HCMC (SGN) to Da Nang (DAD) and transfer to your villa on the Son Tra Peninsula.

Recommendation: Use Grab for transfers on both ends for efficiency and fair pricing.

Da Nang & Environs (Days 5-9)

Day 5 (Dec 24): Son Tra Exploration

Explore Linh Ung Pagoda, Ban Co Peak, and My Khe Beach.

Watch Out For: A respectful dress code (cover shoulders and knees) is required for Linh Ung Pagoda. Remember, My Khe Beach is for a cool, windy walk, not swimming.
Tactical Advantage: Your villa is on the peninsula. Go to Linh Ung Pagoda at 6:00 AM for sunrise. You will have a serene, crowd-free experience that other tourists, who arrive in buses after 8 AM, will not get.

Day 6 (Dec 25): Ba Na Hills

Full-day tour to Ba Na Hills and the Golden Bridge.

CRITICAL WARNING: PEAK SEASON
You are visiting on Christmas Day (Dec 25), which is a designated "peak season" day and part of the city's Christmas Festival. This guarantees MAXIMUM crowd density and extensive queuing.
Essential Recommendation: You MUST purchase the "Ba Na Hills Wow Pass" (approx. 300,000 VND / ~SGD 16) to skip the cable car lines. This is a critical decision to save 1-2 hours of queuing and preserve the value of your day.

Day 7 (Dec 26): Hoi An & Marble Mtn.

The original plan to split these is inefficient. Marble Mountains is located *between* your villa and Hoi An.

Optimized Plan: Book a "Marble Mountains and Hoi An" combined tour. These tours are economical (SGD ~26-34) and efficient, typically visiting Marble Mountains in the afternoon and then Hoi An for its famous lantern-lit evening and night market.
Watch Out For: Bring cash for three separate fees at Marble Mountains: Main mountain (40k VND), Am Phu Cave (20k VND), and the one-way elevator (15k VND).

Day 8 (Dec 27): Dragon Bridge Show

Free day (since Marble Mtn. was moved), followed by Son Tra Night Market and the Dragon Bridge fire show.

Plan Validation: Your timing is CORRECT. The fire/water show runs at 9:00 PM on Fridays, Saturdays, and Sundays. Arrive at the Son Tra Night Market around 7:30 PM for food, then walk to Tran Hung Dao Street or the Love Lock Bridge by 8:45 PM for the best view.
Market Authenticity:
  • Son Tra Night Market: Good for convenience to the bridge, but it's a "tourist trap" with repetitive stalls and high prices.
  • Han Market (Daytime): This is the true authentic local food experience.
  • Helio Night Market: A superior night market for food variety and a more local vibe, but requires a separate trip.

Day 9 (Dec 28): Departure

Visit Han Market for last-minute souvenirs before flying home from Da Nang (DAD).

Recommendation: Han Market is an excellent choice for this. It's well-known for its dried food section (dried squid, beef jerky, etc.) which are well-packaged for travel.
