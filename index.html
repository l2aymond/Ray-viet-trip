    Must-Try Food & Drinks

    Ho Chi Minh City

    Phở (Pho)

    The world-famous beef (Phở Bò) or chicken (Phở Gà) noodle soup. The HCMC style is typically served with a side platter of fresh herbs, bean sprouts, and hoisin sauce.

    Bánh Mì

    A crunchy French baguette filled with a fusion of flavors: pâté, pickled carrots, cilantro, cucumber, chili, and a choice of meat like grilled pork or cold cuts.

    Cơm Tấm (Broken Rice)

    A Saigon classic. "Broken" rice grains topped with a grilled pork chop (sườn), shredded pork skin (bì), and a steamed egg-pork meatloaf (chả). Often served with a fried egg.

    Cà Phê Sữa Đá (Iced Coffee)

    The iconic Vietnamese iced coffee. Strong, dark-roast coffee from a phin filter drips over sweetened condensed milk, which is then stirred and poured over a full glass of ice.

    Da Nang & Central Vietnam

    Mì Quảng (Quang Noodles)

    Da Nang's signature dish. Wide, flat rice noodles are stained yellow with turmeric and served with a tiny amount of intense broth, pork, shrimp, and peanuts. It's more of a "noodle salad" than a soup.

    Bánh Xèo (Central Style)

    Unlike the large, soft Saigon version, the Central Vietnamese "sizzling pancake" is small, crispy, and often filled with just shrimp and bean sprouts. You wrap it in rice paper with herbs and dip it in a savory peanut sauce.

    Hải Sản (Seafood)

    As a coastal city, Da Nang's seafood is a must-try. Visit a "pick-your-own" restaurant (like the Michelin-listed Be Man) to choose live crabs, prawns, and fish, then have them cooked to order (steamed, grilled, or in tamarind sauce).

    Kem Bơ (Avocado Ice Cream)

    A local Da Nang dessert found in markets (like Han Market). It's a layer of fresh avocado puree, topped with coconut ice cream, and sprinkled with toasted coconut flakes. A surprisingly delicious combination.

    Critical Pre-Departure Briefing

    1. Connectivity Strategy (eSIM)

    The plan to buy a physical SIM at the airport carries a high risk of scams (overcharging, faulty SIMs). This could leave you without data when you need it most for booking a Grab.

    Recommendation: Pre-purchase a Viettel network eSIM from a trusted platform like Klook before you travel. This is the most secure and efficient strategy, ensuring you have data the moment you land.

    2. Climate Expectation (Da Nang)

    December in Da Nang (20-25°C) is not a beach-swimming month. Your notes on "rougher waters and stronger winds" are correct. Swimming will likely not be feasible or safe.

    Recommendation: Reframe your time at My Khe Beach as a scenic walk or photographic opportunity. Pack a light jacket or sweater, as it will be necessary for beach walks and evenings.

    3. Budget Mismatch (Da Nang Seafood)

    There is a critical mismatch between your restaurant budget (150k-400k VND) and your selected restaurants. My Hanh Seafood and Be Man Seafood are Michelin-listed, with actual costs closer to 500k-1,000k VND per person.

    Recommendation: Treat these meals as "splurge" experiences and adjust your daily budget accordingly, or, seek out standard, non-Michelin-listed local restaurants which will fit your 150k-300k VND budget.

    4. Health & Safety Protocols

    • Theft Protocol (HCMC D1 / Ben Thanh): Do not use back pockets. Wear a cross-body bag on your front. Do not walk and text with your phone in hand near the street; motorbike snatching is a real risk.
    • Insect Protocol (Cu Chi Tunnels): This is non-negotiable. Wear long pants and closed-toe shoes. Apply strong mosquito repellent *before* you get on the bus and bring it to re-apply.
    • Food Safety Protocol: Use the 3-step test for street food: Is it busy (especially with locals)? Is it hot (cooked-to-order)? Is it clean (observe the vendor)? Do not drink tap water.
